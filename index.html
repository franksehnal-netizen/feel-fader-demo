    Getting started

    Connect your Feel Fader via USB and click Connect & load. The configurator reads the device config; after editing, click Send to device to apply your changes.

    Faders

    Each fader sends a MIDI CC on a channel you choose (Expression / Dynamics by default). The on-screen faders mirror the hardware — they are display only and cannot be dragged.

    Banks

    Bank names and icons are saved in this browser and — with firmware 1.1.0+ — also on the device, so they follow it between computers. The frosty pill is the bank you're editing; the small controller icon marks the bank currently active on the hardware. Library setups show a review before changing the bank; built-in setups are starting points and should be checked against the exact library patch.

    Roller modes

    Articulation (CC) — the roller steps through articulations (UACC). Keyswitch — the roller sends note keyswitches selected on a piano keyboard (see below). Navigation (keys) — the roller sends a keyboard key per detent (e.g. arrows) to navigate your DAW; requires Keyboard (HID).

    Keyswitch mode

    In Keyswitch mode the roller sends MIDI note keyswitches on the configured channel. Click one piano key or drag across a range; rotating the roller steps through the selected notes. Reorder long sequences by dragging chips or with Alt + arrow keys.

    Button macro (long-press)

    A long-press (≥ 0.5 s) of the device button sends a defined key combo — e.g. play/stop or a DAW shortcut. Requires Keyboard (HID). A short press still cycles banks. The Button Macro panel remains visible while HID is off and offers a direct Enable Keyboard action.

    Keyboard (HID)

    Enable HID (in Device & Settings) to let the device send keystrokes — required for the Navigation roller mode and the button long-press macro.

    MIDI diagnostics

    Open MIDI diagnostics in Device & Settings to inspect the active bank mapping, last received MIDI event, firmware, HID state and config hash, or copy a support snapshot.

    Service: DEV / PROD mode

    Hold the bank button while plugging in USB to boot DEV mode — the FEELFADER drive becomes visible for firmware updates. Plug in normally (button not held) for PROD — the drive stays hidden. Recovery: hold BOOTSEL while plugging USB → RPI-RP2 drive appears.

    Switching banks

    On the hardware, the button cycles through your banks. You can define up to 8 banks.
